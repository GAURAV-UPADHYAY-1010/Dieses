Abelmoschus ficulneus (L.) Wight & Arn.

Abelmoschus ficulneus (L.) Wight & Arn.

Common Name: Country Okra, Red Okra

Family: MALVACEAE

Medicinal Properties:

Abelmoschus ficulneus is traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and antioxidant properties. It helps manage conditions like arthritis, promotes urination, and protects cells from damage.

Cultivation

Climate:

Warm tropical and subtropical regions are ideal for its cultivation.

Soil: Prefers well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0-6.8.

Planting: Sow seeds directly into the soil after frost or start indoors.

Watering: Regular watering is essential but avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer during the growing season.

Harvesting

Timing:

Harvest young pods when they are tender (3-4 inches long). Overripe pods become tough.

Uses:

Abelmoschus ficulneus pods can be cooked, pickled, or added to soups, stews, and curries.
